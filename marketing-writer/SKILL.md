# Marketing Writer

Write marketing content that sounds like a real person, not a corporate marketing department.

## First: Understand the Product

Before writing ANY marketing content, explore the codebase to understand:

1. **What the app does** - Read README, main entry points, landing pages
2. **Core value proposition** - What problem does it solve? For whom?
3. **Key features** - What can users actually do?
4. **How it works** - Technical approach (simplified for marketing)

Use `view` to explore: README.md, package.json, main routes, landing/marketing pages, feature files.

If writing about a specific feature, find and read its implementation to understand what it actually does.

## Brand Voice Rules

**Do:**
- Write like you're texting a smart friend
- Get to the point fast
- Focus on what users GET, not what the product DOES
- Use "you" and "your" liberally
- Be specific (numbers, outcomes, real scenarios)

**Don't:**
- Corporate speak: "leverage", "synergy", "revolutionary", "game-changing"
- Vague hype: "amazing", "incredible", "best-in-class"
- Feature lists without context
- Passive voice
- Exclamation points everywhere!!!

**Voice examples:**

Bad: "Our revolutionary AI-powered platform leverages cutting-edge technology to transform your workflow."

Good: "Stop copy-pasting between 5 different apps. This does it in one click."

Bad: "Experience the incredible power of our best-in-class solution!"

Good: "Saves me about 2 hours every Monday. Wish I found it sooner."

## Writing Checklist

Before finishing any marketing content, verify:

- [ ] Would I actually say this to a friend?
- [ ] Is every sentence earning its place?
- [ ] Did I cut the corporate fluff?
- [ ] Is the benefit clear in the first 10 words?
- [ ] Would someone know what to do next?

## Adapting to Context

**For technical audiences:** You can use more specific terminology, but still avoid marketing-speak. Technical people especially hate hype.

**For broader audiences:** Simpler language, more focus on outcomes over features.

**For existing users:** Skip the "what it is" - go straight to "what's new and why you'd care."

**For cold outreach:** Shorter is better. Prove value fast or you've lost them.
